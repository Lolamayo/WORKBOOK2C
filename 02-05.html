02/04: CAPACITANCE



In class, I made a simple capacitance circuit using two resistors to make an LED light-up when I touch a wire. This works because I am conductive, and the arduino is reading my capacitance, and I am finally closing the electrical circuit by grounding the wire. In the same way, bananas and plants are also conductive due to their high water content. I could plug the wire into the banana and then touch it to ground the circuit and illuminate the LED. I read the numbers on the serial port on the Arduino application to understand how different factors affected the reading of electrical current.

Applicaitons: When the wire was plugged into the banana, the reading on the serial port was lower the further away from the wire I touched the banana / tinfoil. The numbers on the serial port, which correlated to the electrical current, were lower the further away from the power source I made contact. An iteration of this proejct could be to use the switch case to make a different sound play for each reading-increment on the serial port. For example, 400-450 makes one tone, and touching furhter away, 500-550 makes another sound, overall creating a song.
