30/04: SINGING PLANT



I stumbled upon an instructable where Madshobye makes Plants Sing , using a touche shield, going off of the same principle as the theremin. Any 2 conductive objects have a capacitance, and so by connecting a capacitance sensor to a plant we can detect if people are touching it and convert the touch into sound. Normally you would do this with a theremin like interface. Madshobye uses the touché frequency principle by Disney Research lab instead, which runs entirely on the Arduino platform. The Touché works on a similar principle as a normal theremin, but does a frequency sweep instead.

[Components needed:
Arduino, Homemade touche shield [Prototyping shield, Pin headers, Resister: 10k, 1MOmh, 3,3k, Capacitor: 100pf, 10nf, Diode: 1N4148 diode Coil / inductor: 10mH (cypax.dk part no: 07.055.0510)], Gameduino (for sound), Two buttons, 2 alligator clips, 2 banana connectors.]

My first plan was to follow the instructable exactly, just buying the parts it names. I sent it to Karen ann who said there are definitely other more intuitive ways of playing with touch-sensitive capacitance and sound without buying these unnecessary specialist parts. So then, I focussed on this idea of Touch-Sensitive Capacitance & Sound.
I loved how the Singing Plant project turned touch into sound, mixing senses to create one art out a silent act. I want to make an interpersonal human-human version of this project, to make sound from touching. A Love-Song-Love-Machine, that can translate love (touch) into a song.
