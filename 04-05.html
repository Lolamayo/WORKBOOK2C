04/05: David Eagleman



David Eagleman is a neuroscientist who's work is dedicated to sensory substitution and addition, asking the question: Can sensory data be fed through unusual sensory channels? & And can the brain learn to extract the meaning of such information streams? Sensory substitution is a non-invasive technique for circumventing the loss of one sense by feeding its information through another channel. Eagleman has developed this technique to develop method to allow hearing impaired people to perceive auditory information via small vibrations on their skin. An additional application of this theory is rewiring the brain's channels to recognise and recode additional codes to unlock additional senses.
